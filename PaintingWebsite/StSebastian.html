A Closer Look at The San Giobbe Altarpiece, by Giovanni Bellini

Paul Ruffolo, HARC 0209

Saint Sebastian

Located to the right of the Madonna and Child, Saint Sebastian stands casually, arrow wounds clearly visible. The pose he takes is called a contropposto stance, where leans to one side with most of his weight on one leg. This pose contrasts to earlier Renaissance works, where many human figures are posed statically with weight evenly distributed on one foot. This contrapposto stance gives Saint Sebastian a much more lifelike and relatable portrayal [2]. Saint Sebastian's divinity and spirituality and exemplified through the contrapposto pose as well. Despite being grievously wounded from arrows, with blood seeping from the wound on his stomach, he displays no pain or discomfort whatsoever - showing how he has been "released from any hint of physical suffering" [2].
